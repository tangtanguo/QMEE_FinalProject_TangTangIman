# QMEE_FinalProject_TangTangIman
Final Project for BIO708

For this project, we are using behavioural data Tang Tang collected looking at maternal care and sibling interaction behaviours between bat moms and their pups across six developmental time points. She had two conditions: single-pup or double-pup conditions, where mom was either interacting with one of her pups (single-pup condition) or both of her pups (double-pup conditions). We will use this data to address the following biological questions:

RESEARCH QUESTIONS:

(1) How do parental care behaviours (i.e. guidance, duration of attachment, attachment latency) change over pup development? 

If moms invest less in parental care over time, then we predict to see:
- decreased successful attachment attempts to teat
- decreased attachment durations to teat
- decreased guidance of pups towards teat
- increased latency to attach to teat


(2) How do pup behaciours change in the presence/absence of a competitor (i.e. their sibling)?

If there is sibling competition, then in the presence of a sibling, we predict to see:
- decreased successful attachment attempts to teat 
- decreased attachment durations to teat
- decreased guidance of each pup
- increased latency to attach to teat
